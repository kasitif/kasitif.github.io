## Active Research Projects

### Polarimetric SAR for Flood Mapping (PhD Research)
**Institution:** University of Stirling | **Funding:** NERC  
**Duration:** 2022 – 2026

Developing novel methodologies for flood extent mapping using polarimetric Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) data. This research exploits polarimetric information from satellites like Sentinel-1, SAOCOM and ground based SAR data to improve flood detection accuracy in complex environments including vegetated floodplains, urban areas, and wetlands.

**Research Highlights:**
- Novel polarimetric change detection techniques for flood extent extraction
- Multi-sensor flood mapping validation using optical drone and satellite data
- Experimentation with a ground radar to synthesize SAR data and test different flood scenarios in a controlled environment

**Outputs:** 3 conference papers, 1 journal article, open-source processing tools

### Social Media for Disaster Response (SEPA Placement)
**Institution:** Scottish Environment Protection Agency  
**Duration:** February 2025 – July 2025

Designing deep learning models that integrate real-time social media data (Twitter/X) with traditional earth observation for rapid disaster impact assessment. The system extracts geotagged posts related to floods, droughts, and storms to provide near-real-time situational awareness for emergency responders.

**Technical Components:**

- Flood extent mapping using SAR

### Water Hyacinth Monitoring in Lake Victoria
**Institution:** University of Stirling  
**Duration:** 2022 – 2024

Applied a polarimetric SAR-based methodology for monitoring invasive water hyacinth in Lake Victoria. This work addresses a critical environmental challenge affecting 30+ million people who depend on the lake for livelihoods, demonstrating how radar remote sensing can support ecosystem management.

**Key Achievements:**
- First application of dual-pol SAR for water hyacinth detection
- Temporal analysis showing seasonal infestation patterns
- Published in IEEE JSTARS (Q1 journal)
- Collaboration with International Crops Research Institute for the Semi-Arid Tropics (ICRISAT)
- Presented at IGARSS, EUSAR, and InGARSS conferences

**Impact:** Supporting regional efforts to control water hyacinth spread and protect Lake Victoria's ecosystem.

## Operational Systems

### Tanzania Streamflow Monitoring System
**Organization:** SERVIR Eastern & Southern Africa  
**Duration:** 2019 – 2022 (Operational since 2021)  

Led the development of Tanzania's first operational streamflow monitoring and forecasting system. The platform provides real-time and forecasted streamflow for over 50 major rivers, supporting water resources management, hydropower operations, and flood early warning.

**System Features:**
- Variable Infiltration Capacity (VIC) hydrological model
- Integration of satellite precipitation (GPM, TAMSAT, CHIRPS)
- 5-day streamflow forecasts with skill assessment
- Interactive web-based visualization dashboard
- Automated daily processing and dissemination
- API for integration with external systems

**Impact:** Intended to be used by Tanzania's Ministry of Water for water allocation,  hydropower planning and flood warnings.

---

### Rapid Flood Damage Assessment - Western Kenya
**Organization:** SERVIR Eastern & Southern Africa  
**Duration:** 2020

Conducted rapid flood damage assessment following catastrophic flooding in Western Kenya using Google Earth Engine and satellite imagery. The analysis quantified impacts on agriculture, infrastructure, and settlements to support emergency response and recovery planning.

**Methodology:**
- Sentinel-1 SAR for flood extent mapping
- Sentinel-2 optical imagery for damage classification
- Google Earth Engine cloud processing
- Integration with population and infrastructure datasets
- Rapid turnaround (48-hour analysis cycle)

**Impact:** Informed humanitarian response reaching 15,000+ affected households.

### Community-Based Flood Early Warning Systems (CBFEWS) – Malawi

**Organization**: SERVIR Eastern & Southern Africa
**Duration**: 2020 - 2021

Led the implementation of Community-Based Flood Early Warning Systems (CBFEWS) in Malawi to enhance local flood preparedness and response. Conducted flood risk assessments, site scoping, system design, and supervised the installation of telemetric water-level monitoring devices. Integrated ground-based monitoring with satellite-derived streamflow data to improve forecasting accuracy and increase early warning lead time for vulnerable communities.

**Key Contributions**:

- Conducted flood hazard and risk assessments to identify priority intervention sites
- Designed and deployed telemetric river gauge systems for real-time water level monitoring
- Integrated satellite-derived streamflow data into community-level warning frameworks
- Supported local stakeholders in operationalizing early warning protocols
- Strengthened institutional capacity for flood preparedness and response
